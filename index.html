Our Story

  • April - 1st Match.com Date - Met at a restaurant in Arlington. Emily was very quiet during the date. Brian thought something was wrong. Emily was trying to be mysterious. Luckily, that did not scare Brian away.
  • May - 1st Apple Blossom - Emily and Brian wanted to hang out in Winchester for the festival, but that meant that Brian would be introduced to Emily's parents. Even though it was a bit soon for Brian to meet the parents, he was a great sport and decided to come out to Winchester after only dating for a month. The Bishops then proceeded to invite the local FBI agent and his family to dinner to meet Brian as well. Luckily, Brian was still not scared away.
  • May - 1st Caps Game - Emily and Brian attend their first Capitals hockey game together with Brian's sister and brother-in-law. The game was preceded by a meal at FUEL - first of many. A wonderful time was had by all. Emily especially liked that she could cheer with a cowbell instead of yelling. She now brings a cowbell to every game and rings it with zeal.
  • June - RAW Live in DC - Emily had never watched RAW. This event did not change that fact.
  • October - You Like That/1st Redskins Game/1st Caps
  • November - Double Football Weekend - Emily is slowly but surely beginning to understand how much passion Brian has for sports. He talked her into going to Blacksburg and Charlotte in one weekend for double header football games for VT and the Redskins. We lost both games - at least the tailgates were epic.
  • December - 1st trip to Brown City, MI - Emily never thought she'd take anyone to Brown City until she had a ring. But she realized Brian was special, and she wanted him to meet her family - another event that could have very easily scared him away...
  • February - Valentine's/Bday Ski - Emily realizes that Brian knows how to celebrate birthdays. He takes her skiing for her birthday.
  • Arpil - Cherry Blossom Festival - Emily and Brian run the Cherry Blossom 10-miler together in 30 degree weather with 50-60 mile per hour winds. Brian is determined to persuade Emily she is a runner.
  • September - Family Trip Va Beach Half - Brian, Emily, and Charlie run the half marathon a day after hurricane Hermine hit Virginia Beach.
  • October - London/Paris - The only way to get Brian out of the country was to convince him that the Redskins needed him in London to cheer them on. The trip proved to be a wonderful cultural experience. Unfortunately, the game resulted in the only tie football game Emily and Brian had ever seen in person. Emily enjoyed teaching the British the rules of American Football.
  • February - EB Surprise 30/indoor skydive - Once again, Brian makes wonderful birthday plans, complete with a surprise birthday party and an indoor skydive experience.
  • Arpil - 1st Valor Game - Emily convinces Brian that arena football was not a sport worth spending time and money on./Maryland Chicken Wing Festival
  • June - Saw Tebow play baseball/Nashville/Father's Day Top Golf - We had an epic joint Father's Day celebration at Top Golf. Let's just say, we are really glad no one was hurt…
  • November - New Orleans - Emily and Brian eat their way through New Orleans. Not complete without watching the Redskins lose in an epic way on the road.
  • December - Engaged - Brian proposed to Emily on Christmas Day after visiting the National Christmas tree. Brian proposed outside the restaurant where they had their first date. And yes, he got down on one knee. Emily said "Yes!"
  • June - Caps in Stanley Cup Final - The Capitals hockey season is still not over, putting a strain on wedding planning.
  • June - Caps in Stanley Cup Final - The Capitals hockey season is still not over, putting a strain on wedding planning.
  • September 29th, 2018 - Wedding!

The Wedding Party

The Bridesmaids

Caitlin Detwiler - Maid of Honor

I met Caitlin in the fourth grade. We spent most of elementary and high school together. At one point, we even wrote up adoption papers to make it official that she was my sister. I am so thankful for such an amazing friend that has been by my side through everything from awkward preteen years to my present wedding day.

Sarah Wreede

Sarah is my cousin on my mom’s side of the family. Even though we grew up miles apart (she lived in Michigan), we’ve always had a strong bond that has only grown stronger as we have become adults. We used to think we were twins because grandma would buy us matching everything growing up. She’s one of the few people that truly understands my crazy family. I am so excited to spend this special day with her by my side.

Katelyn Bishop

Katelyn is my cousin on my dad’s side of the family. We were fortunate enough to live within an hour and a half of each other growing up, so we spent most holidays together. I always viewed her as my little sister. Some of my fondest memories are when she and her two older brothers would come to our house for a week in the summer and when I would spend time in the big city with her family. I am very proud of the young lady she’s become and excited that she will be standing on the altar with me.

Charlie Costello

Charlie is Brian’s sister. I am very excited to finally have an actual sister! We always have such a great time hanging out with her, her husband Anthony, and my new puppy nephew Baxter. She is a wonderful sister to Brian and has been a needed motivator throughout the wedding planning process. I’m excited for our relationship to grow in the coming years.

The Groomsmen

Bortner - Best Man

The Best))))) Man. It all started with inline hockey, and here we are 20 years later still goofing around playing on Tuesday nights. Man, we’re getting old. Proud to call him a friend as he is a great father and continues to proudly serve our country. Glad we got to go to a Stanley Cup Final game, and I still can’t believe the Caps actually won the Cup.

Moosh

The best gamer that I know. Met at Lake Ridge Middle; in the band of course. From competing against frats in 3-on-3 basketball, to playing Halo marathons into the wee hours of the night (#Beytrayed), to rooming at Windsor Hills fun was usually to be had. Bronk!

Neil

The best disc golfer that I know, and maybe the best beard-grower as well. Some highlights: Brown City Christmases, Redskins’ Tailgates, and the family chat. It’s been great hanging out these past few years, and I still owe you a round of disc golf. You’ve helped make me feel a part of the family from the beginning.

Tony

The loudest screamer that I know, which comes in handy at Caps’ games. From my sister’s secret boyfriend to brother-in-law we’ve had a lot of fun together. Some highlights: epic Men’s Nights, fun beach/lake trips, and inspiring you to become a Caps’ fan. Also, I hope your dead toe comes back to life someday.
